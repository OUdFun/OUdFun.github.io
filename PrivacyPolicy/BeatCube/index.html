1. Scope of this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to Beat Cube on Meta Quest and to the related webpage hosted at https://oudfun.github.io/.

Beat Cube is a paid VR game. The game does not contain advertising, does not use in-app purchases, and does not use subscriptions.

2. Information We Collect

Beat Cube may collect or process the following categories of information:

Meta platform information:

  • Meta User ID
  • Meta username or display name
  • Meta profile picture or avatar, where available through Meta services
  • Leaderboard-related information provided through Meta services

Game data:

  • Player name
  • Leaderboard score
  • Game progress
  • Game settings
  • Unlocks
  • Gameplay statistics

Analytics data:

  • Gameplay events
  • Session information
  • Game performance information
  • Device and app information, such as app version, platform, device model, operating system information, and technical identifiers used by analytics services

Crash and diagnostics data:

  • Crash reports
  • Error logs
  • Device and app information related to crashes
  • Technical identifiers used to diagnose crashes

Website data:

  • The website may use cookies or similar technologies for basic website operation, traffic measurement, analytics, or security.

3. Information We Do Not Collect

Beat Cube does not request or collect microphone data.

Beat Cube does not record, transmit, or store voice or audio.

Beat Cube does not provide voice chat.

Beat Cube does not use hand tracking, body tracking, eye tracking, facial expression tracking, passthrough camera access, spatial room data, location data, Bluetooth data, or shared file storage.

Beat Cube does not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13.

4. How We Use Information and Legal Bases

We use the information described above for the following purposes. Where GDPR applies, the legal basis for each purpose is indicated.

Contract performance (GDPR Article 6(1)(b)) — processing necessary to operate the game for the player:

  • To operate the game
  • To identify the player inside game systems where needed
  • To display leaderboard scores
  • To save and restore game progress
  • To store settings and unlocks

Legitimate interests (GDPR Article 6(1)(f)) — our legitimate interest in maintaining and improving the game:

  • To understand how players use the game
  • To improve game balance, performance, and stability
  • To detect, investigate, and fix crashes or technical problems

Legal obligation (GDPR Article 6(1)(c)) — compliance with applicable legal and platform requirements:

  • To respond to privacy requests
  • To comply with platform, legal, and security requirements

5. Third-Party Services

Beat Cube uses the following third-party services:

  • Meta Platform SDK and Meta services
  • Firebase Analytics
  • Firebase Crashlytics
  • GameAnalytics

These services may process technical, analytics, crash, gameplay, or platform-related information according to their own terms and privacy documentation.

We do not sell user data.

We do not share user data for advertising or cross-app tracking.

We do not use advertising SDKs in Beat Cube.

6. Meta Quest Features

Beat Cube uses Meta Platform SDK features for player identification, leaderboards, and cloud save functionality.

The game may use Meta User ID, username, display name, profile picture, avatar, leaderboard score, and cloud save data to provide these features.

Any analytics or reporting involving Meta platform data (such as Meta User ID, username, profile picture, or avatar) is conducted only in aggregated, de-identified, or anonymized form, and is used solely to operate and improve the game experience.

Beat Cube does not use Meta achievements.

Beat Cube does not provide multiplayer, matchmaking, rooms, parties, invites, or social gameplay features.

7. Data Storage

Some data is stored locally on the player's device.

Some data may be processed or stored through Firebase, GameAnalytics, and Meta services.

Locally stored data may include settings, progress, unlocks, and other game-related information.

Cloud or service-based data may include leaderboard information, cloud save information, analytics events, crash reports, and gameplay statistics.

8. Data Retention

We generally retain game-related user data for up to 1 year, unless a shorter or longer retention period is required for technical, security, legal, or platform-related reasons.

Retention periods for third-party services:

  • Firebase Crashlytics: crash stack traces and reports are retained for up to 90 days.
  • Firebase Analytics: event data is retained for up to 14 months, depending on the retention configuration applied.
  • GameAnalytics: player and event data is retained according to GameAnalytics' data retention policy, available at https://gameanalytics.com/privacy/.
  • Meta services: data processed through Meta Platform SDK is retained according to Meta's data retention policy, available at https://www.meta.com/legal/privacy-policy/.

Aggregated or anonymized analytics data may be kept longer because it does not directly identify an individual player.

9. Data Deletion Requests

To request deletion of your data, email: ou.dfun@gmail.com
Please include your player name, Meta username, or other information to help us identify the relevant data. We will respond within 30 days.

Where technically possible, we can delete or request deletion of the following data:

  • Player name stored by us
  • Leaderboard-related data controlled by us
  • Game progress stored by us
  • Game settings stored by us
  • Unlocks stored by us
  • Gameplay statistics stored by us
  • Cloud save data controlled by us
  • Firebase Analytics data linked to an available user identifier or app instance identifier, where deletion is technically supported
  • Firebase Crashlytics crash reports linked to a user identifier, where deletion is technically supported
  • GameAnalytics player data where deletion is technically supported and available through the developer tools or support process

Some data may not be possible to delete in a personally identifiable form if it has already been aggregated, anonymized, or separated from direct user identifiers.

Some data may also be retained if required for legal, security, fraud prevention, accounting, dispute resolution, or platform compliance reasons.

Deleting locally stored data from the device may require uninstalling the game or clearing the app's local data on the device.

10. Analytics and Crash Data

Firebase Analytics and GameAnalytics help us understand general gameplay behavior, game performance, progression, and technical usage patterns.

Firebase Crashlytics helps us detect and fix crashes and stability problems.

Analytics and crash data may include technical identifiers, app version, device information, session information, gameplay events, crash traces, and diagnostic information.

We use this information to improve Beat Cube and fix technical problems. We do not use this data for advertising.

Where analytics involves Meta platform data (User ID, username, profile picture, or avatar), such data is used only in aggregated, de-identified, or anonymized form.

11. Children's Privacy

Beat Cube is intended for users aged 13 and older.

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13.

If you believe that a child under 13 has provided personal data to us, contact us at ou.dfun@gmail.com and we will review the request.

Beat Cube uses services that may transfer personal data outside the European Economic Area (EEA). The following transfers may occur:

  • GameAnalytics ApS: headquartered in Denmark (EU/EEA). Data may also be processed in other countries where GameAnalytics or its service providers operate, under applicable safeguards including Standard Contractual Clauses where required. See https://gameanalytics.com/privacy/ for details.
  • Meta Platforms, Inc.: headquartered in the United States. Data transfers to the US and other countries are made under Standard Contractual Clauses or other applicable transfer mechanisms, as described in Meta's privacy policy at https://www.meta.com/legal/privacy-policy/.
